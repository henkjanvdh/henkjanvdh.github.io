Biography

Henk-Jan van den Ham is a researcher at the Erasmus Medical Center. He studies virus-host interactions using -omics technology and bioinformatics. He is particularly interested in immune phenotypes in relation to the outcome of infection and disease. Specific projects include (among others) evaluating the effects of dendritic cell vaccination in chronic HIV infection, helper T cell differentiation & specificity, and understanding the pathogenesis of dengue virus and respiratory syncytial virus (RSV).

Identification of differentially expressed peptides in high-throughput proteomics data

Using a gold-standard dataset and resampling, we evaluated several statistical analysis and imputation procedures for LC-MS/MS data. We show that limma has the highest sensitivity. Imputation reduces sensitivity or inflates the number of false positives. Therefore, we recommend the application of limma on non-imputed data for the analysis of high-throughput proteomics data.
Michiel P. van Ooijen, Victor L. Jong, Marinus J.C. Eijkemans, Albert J.R. Heck, Arno C. Andeweg, Nadine A. Binai, Henk-Jan van den Ham
